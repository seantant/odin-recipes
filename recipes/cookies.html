Chocolate Chip Cookies

Description

I used to beg my mom to let me make chocolate chip cookies for two reasons. First, she had a beautiful blue KitchenAid stand mixer that I always wanted to use, and second, because I loved baking. I chose cookies specifically because I knew she wouldn’t say no to them. It was really a win-win for both of us. Our favorite recipe was the one on the back of the Nestlé Toll House bag of semisweet chocolate morsels. Little did we know that the recipe printed there was steeped in history, a modernized version of the first-ever chocolate chip cookie recipe.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 3/4 cup white sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup chopped nuts
  • 2 (7-ounce) bars semi-sweet chocolate, chopped

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees farenheit and line two large baking sheets with parchment paper
  2. Beat butter, brown sugar, and white sugar together in a large bowl using a hand mixer on low speed. Add eggs one at a time, mixing until combined. Mix in vanilla extract.
  3. Sift flour and baking soda over a medium bowl. Stir in salt. Add to the butter mixture and mix well until combined. Add nuts and chocolate.
  4. Drop rounded tablespoons of dough on the prepared baking sheets.
  5. Bake for 10 to 12 minutes, or until golden brown. Cool slightly before transferring to a wire rack to finish cooling.
